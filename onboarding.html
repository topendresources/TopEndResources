Vehicle Use Agreement
Please read the full agreement before signing
📄 Top End Resources — Vehicle Use Agreement (Ver 2.1)
TOP END RESOURCES PTY LTD — VEHICLE USE AGREEMENT

1. Purpose
This agreement governs the use of all vehicles owned, leased or operated by Top End Resources Pty Ltd (TER). It applies to all employees, contractors and subcontractors who are authorised to drive a TER vehicle.

2. Authorised Use
TER vehicles are to be used for authorised work purposes only. Personal use is not permitted without prior written approval from management. Vehicles must not be used outside designated work areas or after hours unless expressly authorised.

3. Licence Requirements
You must hold a current, valid Australian driver's licence appropriate for the class of vehicle being operated. It is your responsibility to ensure your licence remains current and to notify TER immediately if your licence is suspended, cancelled or downgraded.

4. Pre-Start Checks
Before operating any TER vehicle, you must conduct a pre-start inspection and record any defects or damage using the TER vehicle checklist. Existing damage must be reported and documented before commencing use. Failure to report pre-existing damage may result in the driver being held liable.

5. Fuel & Maintenance
Fuel usage must be logged accurately in the TER Fuel Tracker for every fill. You must not allow the fuel level to drop below ¼ tank. Any mechanical issues, warning lights or concerns must be reported to your supervisor immediately — do not continue operating a defective vehicle.

6. Traffic Laws & Safe Driving
All road rules and speed limits must be observed at all times. Mobile phones must not be used while driving unless via hands-free. Seatbelts must be worn by all occupants. Driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate termination.

7. Passengers
Only authorised TER personnel or clients may travel in TER vehicles. Unauthorised passengers are not permitted under any circumstances.

8. Accidents & Incidents
Any accident, incident or near-miss involving a TER vehicle must be reported to your supervisor immediately, regardless of fault or severity. Do not admit liability at the scene. Complete the TER incident report form within 24 hours.

9. Damage & Liability
Damage caused through negligence, misuse or breach of this agreement may result in the employee being held financially responsible for repair costs. TER insurance does not cover wilful damage or use in breach of this agreement.

10. Consequences of Breach
Breach of this agreement may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment, removal of vehicle use privileges, and/or recovery of costs from the employee.

Banking Card Agreement
Read and acknowledge TER's company banking card use policy
📄 Top End Resources — Company Banking Card Use Agreement
TOP END RESOURCES PTY LTD — COMPANY BANKING CARD USE AGREEMENT

1. Purpose
This agreement sets out the conditions under which a TER company banking card (card) is issued to an employee. The card remains the property of Top End Resources Pty Ltd at all times.

2. Authorised Use
The card is to be used solely for legitimate, approved business expenses incurred in the course of your employment with TER. Authorised expenses include fuel, materials, equipment hire, and other pre-approved operational costs. Personal use is strictly prohibited.

3. Receipt Requirements
A tax invoice or receipt must be obtained for every transaction. Receipts must be submitted to your supervisor or the TER administration team within 48 hours of the transaction. Failure to provide receipts may result in the expense being charged back to the employee.

4. Spending Limits
Transactions are subject to daily and per-transaction limits as advised by TER management. Any purchase exceeding your authorised limit requires prior written approval. Splitting transactions to avoid limits is not permitted.

5. Prohibited Transactions
The card must not be used for: personal purchases, cash withdrawals, gambling, alcohol (unless specifically pre-approved for client entertainment), or any purchase unrelated to TER business operations.

6. Card Security
You are responsible for the security of the card and its PIN at all times. Do not share the card or PIN with any other person. Report a lost, stolen or compromised card to TER management and the issuing bank immediately.

7. Reconciliation
You are required to assist with the monthly reconciliation of card transactions upon request, providing supporting documentation for all transactions within the period.

8. Misuse & Consequences
Unauthorised use of the card, including personal purchases, constitutes a serious breach of this agreement and may constitute fraud. Consequences may include immediate termination, recovery of all unauthorised amounts, and referral to relevant authorities.

9. Return of Card
The card must be returned to TER management immediately upon request, resignation, termination or end of contract. Outstanding balances or disputed transactions remain your responsibility until resolved.
